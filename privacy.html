2. The Notification Listener permission

NotiVault's core function is to capture and store your notifications. To do this, it requires Android's Notification Listener Service permission — a special permission you must enable manually in Android Settings. This is not granted automatically.

With this permission active, the app can read the following from incoming notifications:

  • The name and package identifier of the sending app
  • The notification title and message text
  • The time the notification was received
  • Images attached to notifications (e.g. photos shared via WhatsApp)
  • Sender profile pictures displayed in messaging notifications
You stay in control. You can revoke the Notification Listener permission at any time by going to Android Settings → Apps → NotiVault → Notifications → Remove permission. All previously captured data remains on your device until you delete it.

3. How we use your data

Every piece of data NotiVault collects is used for one purpose only: showing you your notification history inside the app. Specifically, data is used to:

  • Display past notifications organised by app, sender, and date
  • Allow you to search and filter your notification history
  • Enable you to export your data to a file on your own device
  • Auto-delete old notifications according to your chosen retention period

Your data is never used for advertising, behavioural profiling, analytics, or machine learning.

4. Where data is stored

All data is stored locally on your device in the app's private directory — inaccessible to other apps:

  • Notification records — stored in a local SQLite database using Android Room.
  • Images — saved to the app's private internal storage folder.
  • Settings (theme, language, retention period) — stored using Android DataStore.

YunoK Tech has no access to any of this data. We do not run servers or cloud infrastructure for NotiVault.

5. Blocked apps

You can configure a list of apps whose notifications should not be captured. Notifications from blocked apps are ignored at capture time and never stored. You can manage the blocked list in Settings → Blocked apps.

6. Data retention and deletion

You choose how long notifications are kept. Available options are 7 days, 15 days, 30 days, 60 days, or forever. Notifications older than your selected period are automatically deleted by a background cleanup process.

You can also delete all captured data immediately at any time by going to Settings → Clear all data. Uninstalling the app also removes all associated data.

Only the URL itself is sent in this request — no personal data, notification content, or device identifiers are included. This feature operates on your network connection and the data received is stored locally for display purposes only.
